Stay Active

Exercise Tips for Every Day

You don't need a gym membership to stay fit. These simple exercises can be done at home and work wonders for your health.

🚶

Brisk Walking

⏱ 30 min / day

A simple 30-minute brisk walk every day can significantly reduce the risk of heart disease, diabetes, and depression. It's free and needs no equipment.

✅ Burns 150–200 calories per session
🧘

Yoga & Stretching

⏱ 20–45 min / day

Yoga improves flexibility, reduces stress, and supports mental health. Even simple stretches each morning can relieve tension and improve posture.

✅ Reduces cortisol (stress hormone) by 15%
🏋️

Strength Training

⏱ 3x per week

Bodyweight exercises like squats, push-ups, and lunges help build muscle, boost metabolism, and strengthen bones. No gym required.

✅ Increases metabolic rate by up to 7%
🚴

Cycling

⏱ 30 min / day

Cycling is a great low-impact cardio workout that strengthens legs and improves cardiovascular health. It's also an eco-friendly mode of transport!

✅ Improves heart health by 50%
🏊

Swimming

⏱ 30 min / session

Swimming works all major muscle groups and is excellent for people with joint problems. It improves endurance, flexibility, and mental well-being.

✅ Burns up to 500 calories per hour
🤸

Aerobics / Dance

⏱ 20–30 min / day

Fun and energetic aerobics or even dancing at home gets your heart pumping, improves mood, and helps maintain a healthy weight. Great for all ages!

✅ Elevates mood through endorphin release
Stay Hydrated

Daily Water Intake Guide

Water is essential for every cell in your body. The average adult should drink 8–10 glasses (2–2.5 litres) of water every day. Dehydration can cause headaches, fatigue, and poor concentration.

Rest & Recover

Healthy Sleep Habits

Adults need 7–9 hours of quality sleep each night. Poor sleep is linked to obesity, heart disease, depression, and weakened immunity. Follow these steps for better sleep.

1

Set a fixed sleep schedule

Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day — even on weekends. This trains your body clock.

2

Avoid screens 1 hour before bed

Blue light from phones and TVs suppresses melatonin. Read a book or do light stretching instead.

3

Keep your room cool and dark

A dark, cool environment (18–22°C) helps your body enter deep sleep faster and more easily.

4

Avoid heavy meals before sleeping

Eat dinner at least 2–3 hours before bed. Heavy food disrupts sleep and causes acid reflux.

Eat Right

Healthy Food Recommendations

A balanced diet is the foundation of good health. Include a variety of these food groups in your daily meals.

🥦

Vegetables (5+ servings/day)

Spinach, brinjal, carrots, tomatoes, capsicum. Rich in fibre, vitamins, and antioxidants that protect your body.

🍎

Fruits (2–3 servings/day)

Banana, papaya, amla, guava, orange. Packed with vitamins C, A and natural sugars that give clean energy.

🌾

Whole Grains

Brown rice, ragi, jowar, whole wheat chapati. Rich in fibre, they control blood sugar and keep you full longer.

🫘

Pulses & Legumes

Dal, rajma, chana, moong. Excellent plant-based protein and fibre. Great for vegetarians and vegans.

🥛

Dairy (Low-fat)

Milk, curd (dahi), paneer, buttermilk. Good source of calcium and protein. Choose low-fat options.

🥜

Nuts & Seeds

Almonds, walnuts, flaxseeds, chia seeds. Heart-healthy fats and omega-3 fatty acids. A small handful daily is enough.

🚫

Foods to Limit or Avoid

Processed foods, fried snacks, packaged juices, white bread, sugary sodas, excessive salt, and street food cooked in reused oil. These increase risk of obesity, diabetes, and heart disease.

Know Your Body

Understanding Your BMI

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple measure that uses your height and weight to estimate if you have a healthy body weight. It is calculated using the formula below:

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m²)

Example: 70 kg ÷ (1.70 × 1.70) = 24.2

BMI is a screening tool, not a perfect measure of health. Muscle mass, age, and ethnicity can affect results. Always consult a doctor for a complete health assessment.
