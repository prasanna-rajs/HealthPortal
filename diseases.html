Disease Information Center

Learn about common diseases — their causes, symptoms, and how to prevent them. Knowledge is the first step to better health.

💡 Did You Know?

Non-communicable diseases cause 63% of all deaths in India each year.

Diseases like diabetes, heart disease, and hypertension are largely preventable through lifestyle changes, regular health checkups, and early awareness. Scroll down to learn about the most common ones.

Diabetes

Lifestyle Disease

Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body cannot produce or properly use insulin, leading to high blood sugar levels. Type 2 diabetes is the most common form and is closely linked to diet and lifestyle.

Frequent urination Excessive thirst Fatigue Blurred vision Slow healing

✅ Prevention Tips

  • Maintain a healthy body weight
  • Eat a balanced diet low in sugar & refined carbs
  • Exercise at least 30 minutes daily
  • Get regular blood sugar screenings
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol
❤️

Hypertension

High Risk

Hypertension (high blood pressure) is when blood pressure in arteries is persistently elevated. Often called the "silent killer" because it shows few symptoms but can lead to heart attacks and strokes.

Headaches Dizziness Nosebleeds Chest pain Shortness of breath

✅ Prevention Tips

  • Reduce salt intake to less than 5g per day
  • Exercise regularly and stay active
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques
  • Limit alcohol and avoid smoking
  • Monitor blood pressure at home regularly
🦟

Dengue Fever

Infectious

Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral infection spread by the Aedes mosquito. It causes a severe flu-like illness and, in some cases, can develop into a potentially lethal complication called severe dengue.

High fever Severe headache Body aches Skin rash Low platelets

✅ Prevention Tips

  • Eliminate stagnant water around your home
  • Use mosquito repellents and nets
  • Wear full-sleeved clothing outdoors
  • Install window and door screens
  • Seek medical help at first sign of fever
🦠

COVID-19

Respiratory

COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 coronavirus. It primarily affects the respiratory system. Most people recover, but some experience severe illness especially without vaccination.

Fever & cough Loss of smell Breathlessness Fatigue Body pain

✅ Prevention Tips

  • Get fully vaccinated and boosted
  • Wear a mask in crowded places
  • Wash hands frequently for 20+ seconds
  • Maintain social distance when required
  • Isolate immediately if symptoms appear
🫁

Asthma

Respiratory

Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition where the airways become inflamed and narrowed, making it difficult to breathe. Triggers include allergens, pollution, cold air, and exercise.

Wheezing Chest tightness Shortness of breath Coughing at night

✅ Prevention Tips

  • Identify and avoid your personal triggers
  • Keep your home free of dust and pet dander
  • Always carry your prescribed inhaler
  • Avoid smoking and secondhand smoke
  • Do breathing exercises (pranayama) daily
🫀

Heart Disease

Critical

Cardiovascular diseases (CVD) are the leading cause of death globally. Coronary artery disease, heart failure, and arrhythmias are common types. Risk factors include high cholesterol, obesity, and stress.

Chest pain Palpitations Fatigue Swollen legs Irregular heartbeat

✅ Prevention Tips

  • Eat a heart-healthy diet low in saturated fats
  • Exercise regularly to keep heart strong
  • Control blood pressure and cholesterol
  • Stop smoking — it doubles heart disease risk
  • Manage stress and sleep 7–8 hours nightly
⚠️

Important: The information above is for educational purposes only. If you are experiencing symptoms of any of these conditions, please consult a qualified healthcare professional immediately. Do not self-diagnose or self-medicate.

Stay Safe

Common Risk Factors to Watch

Many diseases share the same underlying risk factors. By addressing these, you can reduce risk of multiple conditions at once.

🚬

Tobacco Use

Smoking is linked to heart disease, stroke, lung cancer, and COPD. Quitting at any age helps.

🍟

Unhealthy Diet

High-fat, high-sugar diets contribute to obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular disease.

🛋️

Physical Inactivity

A sedentary lifestyle increases risk of heart disease, diabetes, and certain cancers.

🍺

Alcohol Abuse

Excessive alcohol damages the liver, increases blood pressure, and raises cancer risk.

Know the Signs. Act Early. Save Lives.

Early detection and timely treatment can make a life-saving difference. Get a health checkup today and encourage your family to do the same.
