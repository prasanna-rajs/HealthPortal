🏥 Types of Health Centres

🏘️

Sub-Centre (SC)

First point of contact between the health system and community. Serves 3,000–5,000 people in rural areas. Handles basic maternal and child health services.

🏥

Primary Health Centre (PHC)

Provides curative, preventive, and promotive healthcare to rural populations of ~30,000 people. Handles OPD, lab tests, and inpatient care.

🏨

Community Health Centre (CHC)

Serves ~120,000 people with specialist care including surgery, gynaecology, paediatrics, and medicine. Has 30 inpatient beds.

🏛️

District Hospital

Referral hospital for the entire district. Offers full specialist services, ICU, emergency care, and advanced diagnostics. Fully subsidized for BPL patients.

🩺

Ayushman Arogya Mandir

Formerly Health & Wellness Centres — provide comprehensive primary healthcare including yoga, mental health, and NCD screening. Over 1.7 lakh centres across India.

Questions & Answers

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are answers to the most common questions we receive about health services, this portal, and accessing healthcare in India.

Is the health information on this portal medically verified?

+

Yes. All health information on HealthIndia Portal is based on guidelines from the World Health Organization (WHO), the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare (MoHFW), and peer-reviewed medical sources. However, this portal provides general information only and cannot replace a consultation with a qualified medical professional. Always consult a doctor for personal health advice.

How do I find a government hospital near me?

+

You can find the nearest government health facility by: (1) Calling the National Health Helpline at 104, (2) Visiting the eSanjeevani portal at esanjeevaniopd.in for tele-consultation, (3) Using Google Maps to search "government hospital near me", or (4) Contacting your local ASHA (Accredited Social Health Activist) worker in rural areas.

Are the vaccines listed here available for free in India?

+

Under India's Universal Immunization Programme (UIP), vaccines marked "Free (Govt)" in our schedule are available at no cost at all government health centres, PHCs, CHCs, and district hospitals. Some vaccines for optional or newer diseases may only be available at private clinics at a cost. Consult your doctor or ASHA worker for details on what is available free in your area.

What should I do in a medical emergency?

+

In a medical emergency: (1) Call 102 immediately for an ambulance or 112 for unified emergency services. (2) Keep the person calm and lying down if possible. (3) Do not give any food or water unless specifically instructed. (4) If trained in first aid, perform CPR only if the person is unconscious and not breathing. (5) Rush to the nearest hospital emergency department. Do NOT use this website's contact form for emergencies.

Can I get mental health support online in India?

+

Yes. Several platforms offer free or affordable online mental health support in India: (1) iCall by TISS — free counselling at 9152987821, (2) Vandrevala Foundation — 24/7 crisis support at 1860-2662-345, (3) eSanjeevani — free government tele-consultation portal including mental health, (4) iCall Online Chat at icallhelpline.org, and (5) YourDOST and Wysa for app-based mental wellness support.

How can I contribute to or suggest content for this portal?

+

We welcome feedback and suggestions! Use the contact form above to suggest new topics, report inaccurate information, or propose collaboration. If you are a healthcare professional and would like to contribute verified health content, please email us at info@healthindia.gov.in with your credentials. All submitted content is reviewed by our health information team before publication.

How is the Ayushman Bharat scheme different from regular government healthcare?

+

Ayushman Bharat – Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY) is the world's largest government-funded health insurance scheme. It provides ₹5 lakh per year per family for secondary and tertiary hospitalization to ~10.7 crore poor and vulnerable families. Regular government hospitals offer free or subsidized outpatient care for all. PM-JAY specifically covers hospitalization costs at empanelled government AND private hospitals. Check your eligibility at pmjay.gov.in.
