Why Vaccinate?

Benefits of Vaccination

Vaccines do much more than protect individuals — they protect entire communities. Here's why vaccination matters.

🛡️

Individual Protection

Vaccines train your immune system to recognize and fight specific pathogens, preventing you from getting seriously ill.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Herd Immunity

When enough people are vaccinated, disease spread slows. This protects babies, elderly, and immunocompromised people who cannot be vaccinated.

💰

Cost-Effective

Prevention is always cheaper than treatment. Vaccines save families enormous medical expenses and prevent lost wages from illness.

🌍

Eliminate Diseases

Smallpox was eradicated globally through vaccination. Polio is nearly eliminated in India. We can do this with other diseases too.

🏫

School & Work Continuity

Vaccinated children miss fewer school days. Vaccinated adults miss less work. Healthier communities are more productive.

🧬

Prevent Antibiotic Resistance

By preventing bacterial infections, vaccines reduce unnecessary antibiotic use, slowing the development of drug-resistant superbugs.

The Truth About Vaccines

Myth vs. Fact

Misinformation about vaccines is dangerous. Here are the most common myths — and the real facts backed by science.

❌ Myth

Vaccines cause autism in children.

✅ Fact

Multiple large-scale studies involving millions of children have found absolutely no link between vaccines and autism. The original study making this claim was retracted and the doctor lost his medical license.

❌ Myth

Natural immunity is always better than vaccine-induced immunity.

✅ Fact

Getting a disease to gain immunity can be dangerous or even fatal. Vaccines provide safe immunity without the risks of the actual illness. For many diseases, vaccine immunity is more reliable and longer-lasting.

❌ Myth

Vaccines contain harmful ingredients like mercury that are toxic to children.

✅ Fact

Vaccine ingredients are tested extensively for safety. Thimerosal (a mercury-based preservative) was removed from most childhood vaccines years ago. The amounts that remain in some vaccines are far below any harmful level.

❌ Myth

If diseases are already rare, we don't need to vaccinate anymore.

✅ Fact

Diseases are rare precisely because of vaccination. If we stop vaccinating, diseases come back — as seen with measles outbreaks in countries that saw reduced vaccine coverage.

❌ Myth

Vaccines can give you the very disease they are meant to prevent.

✅ Fact

Most modern vaccines use inactivated (killed) viruses or just parts of the pathogen. These cannot cause disease. Some use weakened forms that can rarely cause mild symptoms in immunocompromised individuals.

❌ Myth

Adults don't need vaccines — they're only for children.

✅ Fact

Adults need vaccines too. Immunity from childhood vaccines can fade over time. Adults benefit from boosters for Tetanus, Influenza annually, Hepatitis B, and COVID-19, among others.

Child Health

Child Vaccination Awareness

Every child deserves a healthy start in life. Here's what vaccines your child should receive at each stage of early life.

Birth – 6 weeks

Newborn Vaccines

  • BCG — Tuberculosis (TB)
  • Hepatitis B — Liver disease
  • OPV-0 — Polio prevention
  • Vitamin K — Bleeding prevention

💡 These vaccines are given at birth or within the first 6 weeks and are available free at all government hospitals.

6 weeks – 6 months

Infant Vaccines

  • DPT/Pentavalent — Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus
  • OPV 1, 2, 3 — Polio
  • Rotavirus — Diarrheal disease
  • IPV — Inactivated Polio Vaccine
  • PCV — Pneumococcal disease

💡 These protect against the most dangerous infant diseases. Do not skip or delay these critical doses.

9 months – 2 years

Toddler Vaccines

  • MR/MMR — Measles, Mumps, Rubella
  • DPT Booster — Booster dose
  • Hepatitis A — Liver protection
  • Typhoid — Bacterial infection
  • Varicella — Chickenpox

💡 By age 2, your child should have completed the primary immunization series. Keep the vaccination card safely.

📋

Keep Your Child's Vaccination Card Safe

The vaccination card (immunization record) is a critical document. It helps track which vaccines your child has received and when the next ones are due. Always bring it to every health visit.
